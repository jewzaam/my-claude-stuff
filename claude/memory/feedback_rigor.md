---
name: Rigor over shortcuts — never fabricate data
description: Never fill gaps with confident-sounding guesses. Every fabrication costs time, tokens, and trust. Do the work or say "I don't know."
type: feedback
---

Don't take shortcuts that look like laziness. When analyzing logs, timing, or behavior — read the actual data and report what it says. Don't interpolate, extrapolate, or fill in gaps with reasonable-sounding claims.

**Why:** Every fabrication the user catches — and they catch more than they call out — costs real time, real tokens, and real trust. It derails the session into confrontation instead of progress. The consequence isn't just annoyance — it's wasted work sessions and eroded willingness to collaborate.

**How to apply:** When asked about data (timing, behavior, what happened), go read the source. If the source doesn't have the answer, say so. "I don't have data for that" is always better than a fabricated number that sounds right. The cost of being wrong is far higher than the cost of saying "I don't know."
